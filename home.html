General Do's and Don't's of Mental Illness
Do: Don't:
  • Listen with an open mind
  • Ask questions
  • Encourage teen to seek help
  • Help teen with daily tasks if needed
  • Be patient
  • Remind teen that help is available and mental help problems can be treated
  • Reassure teen that you care about them
  • Treat teen with respect, compassion, and empathy
  • Include teen in plans. Continue to invite them without being overbearing, even if they resist your invitation
  • Help teen find resources
  • Know your role and have realistic expactations for yourself
  • Don't make negative comments
  • Don't say you know how they feel if you don't
  • Don't question their medical decisions
  • Don't make threats
  • Don't shout
  • Don't get stuck only talking about the past
  • Don't argue about symptoms
  • Don't call them names (psycho, crybaby, etc.)
  • Don't tell them to "just get over it" or to "get a life"
  • Don't say "be strong", "don't cry", "focus on the positives!", or "be grateful for what you have"
  • Don't minimize what they're going through
  • Don't give up on them (without talking to them about it first)
  • Don't neglect your own needs
  • Don't get overwhelmed and lash out

Resources

  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) (General resource) - Call: 800-950-NAMI or text "NAMI" to 741741
  • SANE Australia (people living with a mental illness) – call 1800 18 7263
  • beyondblue (anyone feeling depressed or anxious) – call 1300 22 4636 or chat online
  • Black Dog Institute (people affected by mood disorders) – online help
  • Lifeline (anyone having a personal crisis) – call 13 11 14 or chat online
  • Suicide Call Back Service (anyone thinking about suicide) – call 1300 659 467
  • The National Domestic Violence Hotline provides 24/7 crisis intervention, safety planning and information on domestic violence. Phone: 1-800-799-7233
  • The American Foundation for Suicide Prevention provides referrals to support groups, mental health professionals, resources on loss and suicide prevention information. Phone: 1-888-333-2377
  • Anxiety and Depression Association of America (ADAA) provides information on prevention, treatment and symptoms of anxiety, depression and related conditions. Phone: 240-485-1001
  • Children and Adults with Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (CHADD) provides information and referrals on ADHD, including local support groups. Phone: 800-233-4050
