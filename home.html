General Do's and Don't's of Mental Illness
Do: Don't:
  • Listen with an open mind
  • Ask questions
  • Encourage teen to seek help
  • Help teen with daily tasks if needed
  • Be patient
  • Remind teen that help is available and mental help problems can be treated
  • Reassure teen that you care about them
  • Treat teen with respect, compassion, and empathy
  • Include teen in plans. Continue to invite them without being overbearing, even if they resist your invitation
  • Help teen find resources
  • Know your role and have realistic expactations for yourself
  • Don't make negative comments
  • Don't say you know how they feel if you don't
  • Don't question their medical decisions
  • Don't make threats
  • Don't shout
  • Don't get stuck only talking about the past
  • Don't argue about symptoms
  • Don't call them names (psycho, crybaby, etc.)
  • Don't tell them to "just get over it" or to "get a life"
  • Don't say "be strong", "don't cry", "focus on the positives!", or "be grateful for what you have"
  • Don't minimize what they're going through
  • Don't give up on them (without talking to them about it first)
  • Don't neglect your own needs
  • Don't get overwhelmed and lash out
