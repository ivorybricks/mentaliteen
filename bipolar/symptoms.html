Bipolar Disorder

Symptoms

Bipolar Disorder, formerly known as Manic Depression, is a type of mood disorder. A person with a form of Bipolar Disorder may experience depressive, manic, or hypomanic episodes, the symptoms of which can be found below:

Symptoms of a Depressive State:
  • Weight changes and/or decrease in appetite
  • Problems with concentration, memory, decision-making
  • Low energy, or fatigue
  • Feeling guilt, worthlessness, helplessness
  • Feeling hopelessness, pessimism
  • Loss of interest in once-enjoyable activities
  • Ongoing physical symptoms which do not get better with treatment (i.e. headaches, digestive problems, and chronic pain)
  • Ongoing sadness, anxious, or empty moods
  • Irritable or restless state
  • Thoughts of death or suicide, suicidal tendencies and attempts
Symptoms of a Manic State:
  • Racing thoughts, disconnected way of thinking
  • Ambitious/grandiose beliefs
  • Inappropriate irritability
  • Inappropriate behavior in social settings
  • Increase in sexual desire
  • Increase in talking speed or talking volume
  • Obvious increase in energy
  • Inhibited judgement
  • Less need for sleep as a result of heightened energy
Symptoms of a Hypomanic State:
  • Less need for sleep
  • Intense focus on work projects/projects done at home
  • Elated mood
  • Increase in confidence
  • Increase in productivity, creativity
  • Heightened energy, libido
  • Risky or reckless behavior
  • Risky, pleasure-seeking behaviors
