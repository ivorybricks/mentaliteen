Bipolar Disorder

How to Help

Do: Don't:
  • Be patient with them
  • Ask them questions to know more about their symptoms
  • Learn about their illness
  • Realize that Bipolar Disorder and its symptoms may be different from person to person
  • Encourage them to talk to you, and listen
  • Help them track their mood and other systems, as well as their treatments, milestones, and goals
  • Be patient and understanding during mood episodes
  • Let them know that they have options for treatment
  • Let them know that they are not alone
  • Ignore warning signs or red flags
  • Assume that they will experience the same symptoms that everyone with Bipolar Disorder experiences
  • Dismiss their problems
  • Tell them to, “Get over it.”
  • Discourage them from getting treatment
  • Encourage reckless behavior
  • Look at medication as the only option
  • Completely dismiss medication
  • Buy into stereotypes of the illness
Call 911 or a hotline if they are in a crisis
