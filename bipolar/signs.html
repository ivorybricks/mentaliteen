Bipolar Disorder

Signs

Bipolar Disorder, formerly known as Manic Depression, is a type of mood disorder. A person with a form of Bipolar Disorder may experience depressive, manic, or hypomanic episodes. The signs of a depressive, manic, and hypomanic state to look for in a teenager are listed below:

Signs of someone who is in a Depressive State:
  • They have less of an appetite when eating
  • May show signs of weight gain or loss
  • Have difficulty concentrating
  • Seem forgetful or have other problems with their memory
  • Have problems making decisions
  • Appear to be tired, or have low energy
  • If they have issues with sleep, such as having trouble falling asleep, sleeping too much, or early-morning awakenings
  • Seem less interested in activities which used to be enjoyable for them
  • Have physical symptoms, such as headaches, digestive problems, or chronic pain, which do not get better when treated
  • Seem to always be sad, anxious, or numb
  • Appear to be irritable or restless
  • If they have confided in you about suicidal thoughts or tendencies
Signs of someone who is in a Manic State:
  • They talk about ambitious/grandiose beliefs
  • Act elated at inappropriate times
  • Seem irritable for no reason
  • They act out of place or inappropriately in social situations
  • Seem to talk faster or louder than normal
  • Seem more energetic
  • Seem to be making worse decisions
  • Seem to sleep less because of their high energy
Signs of someone who is in a Hypomanic State:
  • Seem to need less sleep
  • Are more absorbed in work or home projects
  • Seem to be in an elated mood
  • Act more confident
  • Seem to be more productive or creative (for example, they may be getting a lot of work done lately)
  • Show an increase in energy
  • Seem to be reckless or take more risks
  • Seem to take more risks in pleasure-seeking behaviors
